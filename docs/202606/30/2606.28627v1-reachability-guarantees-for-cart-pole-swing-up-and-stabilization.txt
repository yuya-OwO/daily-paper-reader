# Reachability Guarantees for Cart-Pole Swing-Up and Stabilization 

Mohamed Khalid M Jaffar 

Department of Aerospace Engineering University of Maryland, College Park, USA Email: khalid26@umd.edu 

Abstract ‚ÄĒThe cart-pole swing-up is a canonical benchmark for nonlinear control of underactuated systems, yet an end-to-end guarantee linking the global swing-up maneuver to the local stabilizer is seldom formalized. We present a reachability analysis of a switched energy-based/LQR controller that certifies convergence to the upright equilibrium from a compact set of initial conditions. The swing-up law is derived from an energy-error Lyapunov function; canceling the autonomous conservative term yields a strictly sign-definite Lyapunov derivative, and convergence follows from LaSalle‚Äôs invariance principle. We also propose an augmented Lyapunov function to regulate the steady-state cart velocity to zero, for which we establish almost-global convergence. For the controller handoff, a switching region is designed to lie strictly within the LQR region of attraction, formally certifying the swing-up-to-stabilization transition. Nu-merical simulations corroborate the theoretical analysis. 

Index Terms ‚ÄĒCart-pole, energy-based swing-up, reachability analysis, Lyapunov theory, LaSalle invariance, region of attrac-tion, LQR, underactuated systems. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

The cart-pole, or pendulum on a cart, is a classical bench-mark for nonlinear control and planning of underactuated mechanical systems [ 1], [ 2]. It serves as a representative model for many practical systems, such as an overhead crane transporting a load, the pitch dynamics of a rocket with gimballed thrust, fuel slosh in a moving tank, and the balance models used to study bipedal locomotion [ 3]. Its swing-up task: driving the pendulum from the stable hanging equilibrium to the unstable upright equilibrium while keeping the cart near the origin, requires traversal through a highly nonlinear region of the state space, making it a compelling testbed for formal verification. Energy-shaping controllers are the dominant paradigm for swing-up of underactuated pendulums. ňö Astr¬® om and Furuta [ 2]pump energy toward the upright homoclinic level set, whereas Chung and Hauser [ 4] regulate the swing energy as an output. Spong‚Äôs partial feedback linearization of an acrobot [ 5] and a pendubot [ 6] extends the energy-based idea to two-link robots; Lozano et al. [ 7] stabilize the pendulum directly about its homoclinic orbit. A common challenge is ensuring internal stability ‚ÄĒ the energy law governs the pendulum subspace but leaves the cart (or passive joint) position unconstrained. Our

work addresses this gap by proposing an augmented Lyapunov function-based control law that regulates the cart to rest, and presenting a closed-form cart-position bound. A parallel line of work certifies stability regions for con-trollers of locally-linearized systems: Lyapunov sublevel sets computed via sum-of-squares programming [ 8], [ 9] and the LQR-trees framework [ 10 ]. These methods certify regions around equilibria or trajectories but typically assume the state is already delivered into the certified set. This paper supplies that missing link for the swing-up task in a cart-pole, certifying that the energy-based controller deposits the state inside the LQR region of attraction, so that the global maneuver and the local certificate compose into a single end-to-end guarantee. This paper makes three contributions. (i) We derive an energy-based swing-up law whose feedforward cancellation of the autonomous conservative term gives a strictly sign-definite Lyapunov derivative, unlike classical energy-pumping laws where this term is left to vanish asymptotically. (ii) We augment the Lyapunov function to also regulate the cart velocity, yielding an almost-global convergence guarantee with a measure-zero exception set. (iii) We formalize the control switching condition by defining a handoff region contained strictly within the certified region of attraction of the LQR stabilizer, yielding an end-to-end reachability guarantee from a compact set of initial conditions to the upright equilibrium. To our knowledge, this is among the first works to certify the swing-up-to-stabilization transition in a cart-pole system holistically. II. S YSTEM DESCRIPTION AND EQUATIONS OF MOTION

The control objective is a swing-up and stabilization task: starting from the open-loop stable downward equilibrium, bring the pendulum to the unstable upright equilibrium and hold it there, while keeping the cart near the origin. Because the upright equilibrium is unstable and the system is under-actuated (one actuated degree of freedom, two mechanical degrees of freedom), no single linear controller can achieve this globally. The approach decomposes the task into two phases connected by a switching rule: 1) Swing-up phase. An energy-shaping controller, derived from a Lyapunov function based on the total mechanical energy of the pendulum, pumps energy into the system until the pendulum approaches the upright equilibrium. 2) Stabilization phase. Once the state enters a certified neighborhood of the upright equilibrium, switch to an LQR controller that locally stabilizes the nonlinear sys-tem. The two phases are connected through a region of attraction (RoA) argument: the ellipsoidal sublevel set of the LQR Lyapunov function is certified as a forward-invariant subset of the nonlinear RoA, and the switching surface is designed to lie strictly inside this ellipsoid.

We presented an end-to-end reachability analysis for cart-pole swing-up and stabilization, certifying that trajectories from a compact neighborhood of the downward equilib-rium reach the upright equilibrium under a switched energy-based/LQR controller. Canceling the cart-pendulum coupling term through controller design yields a strictly sign-definite Lyapunov derivative, and the convergence proof follows via LaSalle‚Äôs invariance principle. We also introduce an aug-mented Lyapunov function whose control law regulates the steady-state cart velocity to zero. The controller switching region is designed to be contained strictly within the LQR sta-bilizer‚Äôs certified ellipsoidal region of attraction, formalizing the handoff condition between the energy-pumping controller and LQR. The theoretical results are corroborated by numeri-cal simulations. Future work will derive analytical bounds on the swing-up time required before switching, and use them to obtain tighter cart-position bounds than the conservative estimates established here.
